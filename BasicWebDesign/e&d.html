Embellishment
Any sort of decorative feature or detail on a garment.

So you have a base garment. What now? Now, you have complete creative liberty to do whatever you wish. If you want to leave it simple, leave it simple! If you want to become a human disco ball, become a human disco ball! There are many types of embellishment. My favorite is embroidery, the use of different colored thick thread to create a pattern or picture. Another one of my favorites is applique, which is when you take a piece of a different fabric (most often lace), and apply it to the garment. The easiest and most common way to make your project exciting and fun is to use a pre-embellished fabric. Textiles with bright or sparkly patterns are especially eye-catching. Another interesting way you can add intrigue is through layering fabric. I prefer to use a solid base fabric, then to add a sheer overlay.

These practices can be very time-consuming. If you see a highly adorned piece, know that a lot of effort, time and love went into creating it. Some of the most illustrious couture houses create items that can take up to 7,500 hours. That's over two years of work spent on just one dress! There is a reason highly decorated garments are so expensive. In fact, the worldwide embroidery market is valued at almost three billion dollars, with millions of employees working hard.
