In this day and age, we are lucky enough to have access to so many tools and supplies that make sewing faster and simpler. We are no longer limited to a needle, some thread, and a piece of fabric, though I find hand sewing to be very enjoyable.

Below is a comprehensive list of the basic tools used to start sewing. These are not all necesities, but are useful to have, and make sewing a fun experience. You can find all of these things at your local crafting store.

  • Fabric Scissors
    • Shears
    • Pinking Shears
    • Snips (tiny scissors for thread)
    • Rotary cutter
  • Needles & Pins
    • Machine needles
    • Size 8-19 needles
    • Straight pins
    • Safety pins
  • Thread
    • Any and all colors!
    • Cotton, linen and silk thread
  • Fabric
    • Natural fibers, such as cotton, silk and wool
    • Synthetic fibers, such as jersey, rayon, and modal
  • Marking utensil
    • Tailor's chalk
    • Erasible pens, heat or water soluble ink
  • Measuring Tape
  • Sewing Machine
